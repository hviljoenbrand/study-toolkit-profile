Study Toolkit Profile

A study-skills check-in

You're using a beta version of the Study Toolkit Profile. We will continue to review and refine it as we go.

This is a check-in on how you study. Studying well is not one big skill, it is a handful of smaller ones that work together, grouped into five areas. There are no right or wrong answers and nothing to pass or fail. All it asks is that you answer honestly, so we can show you what is already working well and where a little more attention would help most. These are habits, and habits can be built.

For each statement, choose the option that best matches how often it is true for you. When you submit, your profile appears on screen to read and keep.

Privacy notice

Who we are

The Study Toolkit Profile is provided by Study Toolbox. The learner, or their parent or guardian, can reach Study Toolbox at info@studytools.co.za or at www.studytools.co.za. For the purposes of South Africa's Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA), Study Toolbox is the responsible party for the learner's information.

What we collect

When the learner completes the profile, Study Toolbox collects the learner's first name, surname, school, grade, the date of completion, the answers given to all 37 statements, and a parent or guardian's email address. For research purposes, Study Toolbox also collects the learner's gender, province or region, and home language.

Why we collect it

This information is used for two things. First, and most importantly, it is used to create the learner's study profile and show it to them on screen, to keep a copy so there is a record, and, where a counsellor or teacher is running the assessment with the learner, to share the profile with them.

Second, the learner's answers, together with their gender, province or region, and home language, may also be used for research purposes, to help review and improve the Study Toolkit Profile over time, including checking that it works fairly and consistently across different learner groups. Where information is used for this purpose, it is analysed in aggregate and is not linked to an identifiable individual. Study Toolbox does not sell the learner's information, and does not share it with any outside party for their own purposes.

Where it is kept

The learner's information is stored securely using Google Workspace, under a data processing agreement that requires Google to protect it to a standard equivalent to POPIA's requirements. Where information is stored or processed outside South Africa as part of this service, it remains covered by that same agreement.

Who can see it

Only Study Toolbox, and, where relevant, the counsellor or teacher running the assessment with the learner. No one else.

How long we keep it

Study Toolbox keeps the learner's information for 12 months, and then deletes it. The learner, or their parent or guardian, can ask for it to be deleted sooner.

Permission for under-18s

Most learners completing this are under 18. For that reason, a parent or guardian must give permission before the assessment is completed.

Your rights

The learner, or their parent or guardian, can ask what information is held, ask for it to be corrected, or ask for it to be deleted, and can object to how it is used. Contact info@studytools.co.za. If not satisfied with the response, the Information Regulator of South Africa can also be contacted.

Marketing

The parent or guardian's email will not be used to send marketing unless they have separately chosen to receive it.
